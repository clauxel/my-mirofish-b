MiroFish trading

MiroFish trading research starts with scenario simulation, not price prediction.

For trading teams and independent researchers, MiroFish is most useful when the question is about narratives, participant behavior, and second-order reactions around a market event.

Build a trading scenario Read Polymarket guide
MiroFish is a research and simulation tool. It is not financial advice, a broker, a trading bot, or a guaranteed signal generator.

Where it helps

Markets move through stories as much as through numbers. MiroFish helps you rehearse how different groups might interpret a catalyst before you make a decision.

Useful inputs include news summaries, policy drafts, earnings notes, market commentary, social posts, or a short thesis about what you believe the market is underpricing.

Example prompts

  • Simulate how retail traders, institutions, and skeptics react to this announcement over 10 rounds.
  • Identify the narratives that could strengthen or weaken the bullish case.
  • Model what changes if a regulator issues a warning tomorrow.

Trading research use cases

Sentiment rehearsal

Test whether a market story attracts agreement, skepticism, confusion, or polarization.

Catalyst analysis

Simulate how a rate move, product launch, earnings miss, lawsuit, or market rumor could spread.

Risk review

Ask which assumptions in your thesis are fragile and which actors are most likely to break them.
