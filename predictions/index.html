Every forecast page is written around the question MiroFish is trying to answer, not around the event as a generic topic. The event is the test case; MiroFish is the product story.

Forecasts are educational product examples. They are not financial, betting, or investment advice.

Why this helps MiroFish users

  • It shows how to turn a public event into a precise prediction question.
  • It demonstrates how MiroFish can separate market consensus, narrative momentum, expert signals, and uncertainty.
  • It gives visitors a concrete prompt they can reuse inside their own workspace.
  • It creates a public record that can be reviewed after each event resolves.

Prompt pattern

Use this shape in MiroFish: "Predict [outcome] for [event]. Compare market odds, official constraints, public attention, recent news, and the catalysts that could change the forecast before resolution."
